One story to inspire

Nobody is perfect, and I'm no-exception! I'm so far from an ideal student, if they exist at all.

In Russia, children have to learn a foreign language from the fifth grade of elementary school. So, I’ve studied English since I was 10 years old. Nowadays I realize that that process was so far from studying—effective studying I mean.

It is unlikely that simply rewriting grammatical structures and a teacher who does not speak English well can teach you anything besides the phrase “HELLO! MY NAME IS VITALINA!”

Then, when I graduated from school, I went to university where I was also required to study English.... and the same story with writing-off, (I don’t know what writing off is?) prayers about not being asked (something here, to speak, questions) in class.

Honestly, all these years were just a waste of time. Approximately 13 years!

I might never have spoken English had it not been for one event...

In 2014, after university graduation, I went to an English camp somewhere near Krasnodar. It was organized by American guys and I still thank them from the bottom of my heart. Guys, you changed my life, you showed me another world where people from different countries can all speak one language.

Since then I was motivated to learn—to speak English. Now I knew it was possible to speak other languages because I saw other Russians speaking English! If they can, why can't I?

This how I began selfstyling: (I don’t know what selfstyling is? Maybe studying by myself?) looking for English speaking partners, endless Skype calls abroad, language courses, speaking clubs, where I would say that my job would somehow will be connected with English.

(Note: At that very moment I was an accountant in a small local company. Hence, such statement about foreign language sounded like a pipe dream.)

However, I decided English would be my profession, and it inspired me so much that five years later I ended up in London.
